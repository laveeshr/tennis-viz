Assignment #2: Interactive Visualization Exercise

The given visualisation provides a comprehensive description of the 10 years Australia Open Matches based on the number of players per country using interactions.

It provides a set of interactions to portray the complete story.
  • Initially a world map is loaded into view depicting the number of players that each country has.
  • By clicking a certain country, all the players of the selected country are listed alongwith their total number of wins in the 10 years span of data.
  • By selecting a player, a diverging graph loads up the comparison of the average of the key aspects of the player Vs the average of the key aspects of his opponents in all the matches that the player won.
  • This graph provides an insight into the parameters that promoted the wins of the player and also the parameters that the player needs to improve inorder to win against his opponents.
  • By selecting another player or another country at any time re-renders the graph in a smooth transition making it dynamic by nature.
  • If any key attribute is 0 for a player and his opponent, then that attribute is skipped in the graph.
  • All the charts are equipped with tooltips to help the viewer get a better understanding of the data.

The key aspects used to visualise the player's strengths and weaknesses.
Key Aspect Type Reason
Ace Service Variable Ace is a good serve that was not received by the opponent. This is an important measure as aces lead to winner points.
AvgFirstServe Service Variable This is the Average of the first serve in KPH. Important to score as first serve determines the pace of the game.
AvgSecServe Service Variable This is the Average of the second serve in KPH. This is also important as it signifies that the first service was a bust but the player picked up the pace with the second serve.
FastServe Service Variable This is the fastest serve served in the game in KPH. Speed plays an important role in tennis as a fast serve maybe harder for the opponent to return leading to an Ace.
Double Service Variable This is the fault on service point where the player fails to serve inside the service box on both service chances. This is an important factor as it signifies that the player lost a whole service point and subsequently a match point.
Error Match Performance Variable This is the point of mistakes. Errors are important to tally as they let us know about the areas where the player needs to improve.
Total Match Performance Variable This is the total points won of the game. Total points won in the game might also include the points that were lost by the opponent and not just won by the player.
Winner Match Performance Variable These are the points won that were not received by the opponent. This is important to note as they signify the points where the player's game was the strongest since the opponent couldn't return the ball.
